Prospective Students

I’m always happy to hear from motivated and enthusiastic students interested in my research areas. However, like most professors, I receive a high volume of emails and cannot respond to every inquiry. To increase the chances of your message receiving a response, please carefully read the following:

Email subject: Start your subject line with [prospective-student] so I know you've read these instructions.

Be specific: Generic emails are likely to be ignored (and may land in spam). Instead, mention which of my recent research topics or publications caught your interest and why.

Show relevance: Highlight how your previous research or skills align with mine. Include links to papers, code, or other concrete contributions if possible.

Have a proposal? If you already have an idea or direction in mind, share a brief proposal. That’s always a bonus.

If you haven’t worked in my research areas before, that’s okay—but make sure your message shows genuine interest and effort to understand what I do. Due to time constraints, I may not be able to reply to every email. Please don’t take it personally. Wishing you the best in your research journey!
